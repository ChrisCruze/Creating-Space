# The Decluttered Space Operating System

> "**What do I do, and how often, so my space never quietly degrades again?**"

## Purpose

This document replaces motivation with **defaults**. It prevents regression by defining ongoing maintenance behaviors. This is about **time and behavior**, not physical inventory. For tracking what you own and where it lives, see [[Inventory-Map]].

## Key Principles

- **Focus on cadence, not perfection** - Regular small actions beat occasional big efforts
- **Defaults over decisions** - Pre-defined behaviors eliminate choice fatigue
- **Prevention over reaction** - Stop clutter before it accumulates
- **Time-bound actions** - Every task has a clear duration limit

## The Five Essential Questions

### 1. What do I do every day to contain entropy?

**Purpose**: Stop objects from becoming permanent clutter through a non-negotiable daily reset.

**Time Limit**: 10-15 minutes maximum

> ‚õî If it takes more than 15 minutes, it's too big. Break it down or move it to weekly.

#### Manhattan Apartment Daily Reset

- **Kitchen counters**: Clear all non-active items before bed
- **Entryway**: Return items to their homes (keys, bags, mail)
- **Horizontal surfaces**: Clear dining table, desk, coffee table
- **Dishwashing**: Run dishwasher if full, empty if clean
- **Mail/paper**: Sort immediately - trash, action, file

**Template**:
- [ ] Clear kitchen counters (5 min)
- [ ] Reset entryway (2 min)
- [ ] Clear horizontal surfaces (5 min)
- [ ] Handle mail/paper (3 min)

**Success Metric**: Can complete in under 15 minutes without rushing

### 2. What do I do every week to maintain function?

**Purpose**: Focus on horizontal surfaces, incoming items, and light resets that prevent accumulation.

**Anchor Time**: Choose a consistent day/time (e.g., Friday PM, Sunday PM)

#### Manhattan Apartment Weekly Maintenance

- **Donation bin**: Empty every Sunday (take to building donation area or schedule pickup)
- **Incoming items review**: Process all new items from the week
- **Closet sweep**: Return any clothes that migrated to chairs/floors
- **Refrigerator**: Remove expired items, wipe down shelves
- **Bathroom**: Clear countertops, organize products
- **Under-bed storage check**: Ensure bins aren't overflowing

**Template**:
- [ ] Empty donation bin (10 min)
- [ ] Process incoming items - assign homes or decide (15 min)
- [ ] Return migrated items to closets/drawers (10 min)
- [ ] Refrigerator cleanout (10 min)
- [ ] Bathroom reset (10 min)
- [ ] Under-bed storage check (5 min)

**Total Time**: ~60 minutes weekly

**Success Metric**: No items accumulate in "temporary" locations for more than a week

### 3. What do I do monthly or quarterly to prevent long-term creep?

**Purpose**: Treat like preventive maintenance. Catch items that became dead weight before they become permanent clutter.

#### Manhattan Apartment Monthly/Quarterly Reviews

**Monthly (First Sunday of Month)**:
- **Closet inventory**: Remove items not worn in 30 days (unless seasonal)
- **Drawer audit**: Identify items that migrated to wrong drawers
- **Storage bin review**: Check under-bed, closet top shelves
- **Digital declutter**: Photos, files, apps

**Quarterly (Every 3 Months)**:
- **"What became dead weight?"** review - Items that seemed useful but aren't
- **Seasonal transition**: Rotate clothing, swap decor if applicable
- **Deep horizontal surface reset**: Move furniture, clean behind/under
- **Storage unit review** (if applicable): What's at sister's place? Still needed?

**Template - Monthly**:
- [ ] Closet inventory - remove unworn items (20 min)
- [ ] Drawer audit - return items to correct homes (15 min)
- [ ] Storage bin check (10 min)
- [ ] Digital declutter (15 min)

**Template - Quarterly**:
- [ ] "Dead weight" review - identify unused items (30 min)
- [ ] Seasonal transition (if applicable) (20 min)
- [ ] Deep surface reset (30 min)
- [ ] Storage review (if applicable) (20 min)

**Success Metric**: No item stays "unresolved" for more than 3 months

### 4. What is my long-term plan?

**Purpose**: Track progress with milestones, not tasks. Define what success looks like over time.

#### Manhattan Apartment Long-Term Milestones

**6-Month Goals**:
- Reduce total item count by 50%
- All horizontal surfaces clear except active-use items
- All closets/storage at 70% capacity or less
- "After" photos that show transformation
- Maintenance routine successfully executed for 4 consecutive weeks

**12-Month Goals**:
- Sustain minimalist lifestyle without regression
- Zero "unresolved" items (everything has a home or is gone)
- Cleaning takes 20 minutes instead of 2 hours
- Friends comment on peaceful space
- Ready for VanLife transition (if applicable)

**Progress Tracking**:
- Monthly photo documentation
- Item count reduction percentage
- Time-to-clean measurements
- Weeks of successful maintenance routine

**Success Metric**: Measurable progress toward milestones, not perfection

### 5. How do I make decisions when I'm unsure?

**Purpose**: Define clear, written decision rules that prevent emotional backsliding and decision paralysis.

#### Decision Rules for Manhattan Apartment

**The Core Questions** (in order):
1. **"Have I used this in the last 6 months?"** (unless seasonal)
2. **"Does this bring genuine joy or serve a clear purpose?"**
3. **"Would I buy this again today at full price?"**
4. **"Does this have a specific home in my space?"** (see [[Inventory-Map]])
5. **"If I needed this, could I replace it easily/cheaply?"**

**Specific Rules**:

- **"One in, one out"**: When buying something new, donate/discard one similar item
- **"No home = no keep"**: If an item doesn't have a designated space, it goes (unless you create space for it)
- **"6-month storage expiration"**: Items in storage bins get reviewed every 6 months - if unused, they go
- **"Horizontal surface rule"**: Nothing lives permanently on horizontal surfaces except active-use items
- **"Duplicate elimination"**: If you have multiples, keep the best one, donate the rest
- **"Sentimental limit"**: One small box maximum for truly sentimental items

**When Unsure**:
1. Put item in "unresolved" bin (see [[Inventory-Map]])
2. Tag with review date (no more than 3 months out)
3. If still unresolved at review date, it goes

**Success Metric**: Decision-making takes under 2 minutes per item using these rules

## Manhattan-Specific Considerations

- **Small spaces** require daily containment - clutter becomes overwhelming faster
- **Vertical storage** needs weekly checks to prevent overflow
- **Limited closet space** means monthly reviews are essential
- **High-density living** means items can't "hide" - they need homes
- **Building amenities** (donation areas, mail rooms) should be part of weekly routine
