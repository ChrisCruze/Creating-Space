Creating Space

Systematic apartment decluttering toward minimalism using proven frameworks. Transform cluttered living space into a calm, spacious environment that supports minimalist lifestyle and prepares for VanLife transition.

Brief

Problem: Apartment has accumulated excessive clutter creating physical and mental friction; current state blocks transition to minimalist living and eventual VanLife setup; lack of systematic decluttering approach leads to decision paralysis and ineffective attempts.

What I'm Solving: Systematically reduce apartment contents to essentials using proven minimalist frameworks while maintaining visibility of stored items and tracking progress objectively; build sustainable habits to prevent clutter return.

Purpose

Reduce apartment clutter by at least 50%, establish a minimalist lifestyle with sustainable maintenance systems, and prepare for eventual VanLife transition. Create a calm, spacious living environment where everything has a designated place and cleaning takes 20 minutes instead of 2 hours.

Cleaning takes 20 minutes instead of 2 hours. Feel liberated rather than weighed down by possessions. Friends visit and comment on how peaceful the space feels. No longer avoid having people over.

Success Criteria

The project is complete when:

  6. Physical Space Metrics: All horizontal surfaces clear except active-use items, all closets/storage at 70% capacity or less, floor space visible and walkable throughout.

Method: KonMari Framework

Following Marie Kondo's method, decluttering proceeds by category (not location) in this order:

  1. Clothes (including underwear, outerwear, jewelry, accessories, bags)

    Gather ALL clothes from entire house including out-of-season. Touch each item, ask "Does this spark joy?" Fold vertically in rectangles that stand up.

  2. Books

    Assemble all books from entire house. Touch to "wake them up" but don't open. Question: "Will this book help me on my journey ahead?"

  3. Papers

    Goal: radical reduction, very little paper should remain. Categorize, then organize by category and alphabetically if keeping.

  4. Komono (Miscellaneous Items)

    Everything else except sentimental: kitchen items, bathroom products, cleaning supplies, tools, electronics, home decor, craft supplies. Gather by sub-category, see everything at once.

  5. Sentimental Items (last, hardest)

    Photos, mementos, heirlooms. Take photos out of albums, go through one by one. Keep only what truly sparks joy. By this point, decision-making skills are honed.

Core Principles

  • Tidy by category, NOT by location
  • Gather everything from that category from entire home before deciding
  • Hold each item, use physical touch to assess connection
  • Ask "Does this spark joy?" - pay attention to body response
  • Thank items before discarding (gratitude vs. guilt mindset)
  • Discard first completely, THEN organize what remains

Manhattan-Specific Strategies

  • Maximize vertical space with shelves, wall-mounted organizers
  • Multi-functional furniture essential: storage ottomans, under-bed storage
  • "One in, one out" rule: when buying something new, donate/discard one similar item
  • Clear surfaces principle: keep countertops, tables, desks minimal
  • Daily tidying ritual: 5-15 minutes end-of-day reset
  • Designated drop-off zone near entrance
